# The Descent of Elturel

## Summary

The Descent of Elturel was a catastrophic event in which the holy city of Elturel was physically ripped from the Material Plane and pulled into Avernus, the first layer of the Nine Hells.

### Details

**Type:** Disaster / Planar Event
**Date:** 1492 DR
**Region/Location:** Elturgard / Avernus
**Participants:** Citizens of Elturel, the Hellriders, Zariel, the party

## Overview

### What happened

Due to a hidden pact made decades prior by High Overseer Thavius Kreeg, the city of Elturel was condemned to the Nine Hells. The city's legendary protector, the Companion (a second sun that hovered over the city), turned black, emitting lightning that bound the city in chains and dragged it into the River Styx.

### Outcome

Thousands of citizens were trapped in Hell, facing corruption and endless sieges by devils and demons, prompting a massive refugee crisis in neighboring Baldur's Gate.

## Chronology

### Beginning

Thavius Kreeg fled to Baldur's Gate in secret. Shortly after, the Companion darkened, and massive iron chains shot from the earth, dragging the city underground.

### Middle

Refugees flooded the roads to Baldur's Gate, where they were met with closed gates and suspicion.

### End (Ongoing in Transcript)

The party successfully descended into Avernus themselves, locating the floating ruins of Elturel suspended over the River Styx (Session 22).

## Consequences

### Immediate effects

- Total collapse of the nation of Elturgard.
- A massive influx of desperate refugees into Baldur's Gate, causing severe political instability.

### Long-term effects

- The party is currently attempting to break the infernal chains holding the city before it is dragged completely into the Styx, which would damn all souls aboard (Session 24).
