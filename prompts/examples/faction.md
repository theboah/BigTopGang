# The Harpers

## Summary

The Harpers, also known as Those Who Harp, are a semi-secret, decentralized organization dedicated to promoting good, preserving history, and opposing tyranny across Faerûn.

### Details

**Type:** Secret Society / Order
**Status:** Active
**Base of Operations:** Decentralized (Notable hubs include Twilight Hall in Berdusk)
**Territory:** Faerûn (Heavily active in the Sword Coast and the North)
**Alignment:** Typically Good (often Chaotic Good)
**Leadership:** The High Harpers

## Description

### Goals

To maintain the balance between civilization and the wild, preserve art and history, and prevent any one faction, empire, or evil entity from accumulating too much power.

### Methods

They operate primarily through espionage, sabotage, and subtle manipulation rather than brute military force. Harper agents often pose as bards, merchants, or traveling clerics.

### Identity

Their symbol is a silver harp overlaid with a crescent moon and stars. They value independence, relying on a network of safehouses and secret signs (like Harper pins).

## History

### Origin

The Harpers have been disbanded and reformed multiple times throughout history, originally tracing their roots back to the elven courts of Myth Drannor and the endorsement of deities like Mystra, Oghma, and Selûne.

### Notable events

- The party uncovered a Harper spy ring operating out of the Baldur's Gate upper city (Session 14).
- A Harper agent, Leosin Erlanthar, provided the party with funds to investigate the Cult of the Dragon (Session 15).

## Membership

### Members

- **Leosin Erlanthar:** Half-elf monk and field agent.
- **Remallia Haventree:** High Harper stationed in Waterdeep.

### Rivals

- The Zhentarim
- The Cult of the Dragon
- The Red Wizards of Thay

## Influence

### Resources

Extensive spy networks, hidden caches of magical items, and strong ties to good-aligned temples and local nobility.

### Reach

Global, though their presence relies on individual cells rather than a unified army.
