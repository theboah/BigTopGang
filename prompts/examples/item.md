# Aegis-fang

## Summary

Aegis-fang is a legendary magical warhammer forged for the barbarian hero Wulfgar by his adoptive father, the dwarven king Bruenor Battlehammer.

### Details

**Type:** Weapon
**Rarity:** Legendary
**Item type:** Warhammer
**Attunement:** Required (Requires high strength/dwarven or barbarian lineage)
**Owner:** Wulfgar

## Description

### Appearance

A beautifully crafted dwarven warhammer with a mythril-inlaid head. The weapon is perfectly balanced, despite its massive size. The symbols of the dwarven gods Moradin and Dumathoin are etched into the sides, along with the crest of Clan Battlehammer.

### Properties

It is a heavy, magical +3 warhammer. Whenever it is thrown, it magically reappears in the wielder's hand. It strikes with enough kinetic force to shatter stone and knock giants off their feet.

## History

### Origin

Bruenor Battlehammer forged the weapon in the fires of the dwarven smithies using pure mithril and a diamond tip, channeling his deepest magic and love for his adopted human son, Wulfgar.

### Notable events

- The party discovered Aegis-fang temporarily unguarded in the vaults of Mithral Hall (Session 19).
- The barbarian player used it to cave in a tunnel, sealing off a horde of drow pursuers before returning it to Wulfgar (Session 20).

## Use

### Function

Used in frontline combat to deal massive bludgeoning damage. Its returning property makes it highly effective at mid-range against flying or fleeing targets.

### Limitations

The weapon is exceptionally heavy and requires immense physical strength to wield effectively. Only those attuned to it can summon it back after throwing it.

## Relationships

### Associated characters

- **Wulfgar:** Primary wielder.
- **Bruenor Battlehammer:** Creator.
- **The Party Barbarian:** Temporarily wielded it during the escape from Mithral Hall.
